Intermediate English Fluency (B1-B2) - Course Application for 2025-2026

This course is for learners who have a foundational understanding of English and wish to develop greater fluency, accuracy, and confidence in a wider range of everyday and more complex situations. You will expand your vocabulary, master more intricate grammar, and improve your ability to understand and participate in discussions on familiar topics. Our interactive online format is tailored to help Spanish speakers overcome common challenges at this level.

Course Details:

  • Start Date: September 15, 2025
  • End Date: June 19, 2026
  • Duration: Approximately 40 weeks, 5-7 hours per week (including live sessions, group work, and self-study)
  • Instructor: Experienced ESL instructors specializing in intermediate learners. (e.g., Mr. David Miller, CELTA Certified)
  • Format: Fully online, featuring engaging live Zoom discussions, collaborative projects, video-based lessons, and practical exercises on our LMS.
  • Language Support: Clarifications for complex topics may be provided in Spanish if needed, but English is the primary language of instruction.
  • Materials: Digital coursebook, supplementary online resources, access to a library of graded readers, and interactive grammar tools are included.

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand the main points of clear standard input on familiar matters regularly encountered in work, school, leisure, etc.
  • Deal with most situations likely to arise whilst travelling in an area where the language is spoken.
  • Produce simple connected text on topics which are familiar or of personal interest.
  • Describe experiences and events, dreams, hopes and ambitions and briefly give reasons and explanations for opinions and plans.
  • Improve pronunciation and intonation for clearer communication.
  • Expand vocabulary to include more abstract and nuanced terms.
  • Master key intermediate grammar structures (e.g., conditional sentences, passive voice, reported speech).

Course Outline (Example Modules):

  1. Module 1: Narrating Past Events & Storytelling
  2. Module 2: Describing People, Places & Experiences
  3. Module 3: Making Plans & Future Arrangements
  4. Module 4: Expressing Opinions, Agreeing & Disagreeing
  5. Module 5: Understanding Cultural Nuances
  6. Module 6: Work & Career-Related English
  7. Module 7: Discussing Current Events & Media
  8. Module 8: Problem Solving & Giving Advice
  9. Module 9: Exploring Hypothetical Situations
  10. Module 10: Consolidating Skills & Advanced Preparation

Entry Requirements:

  • Completion of a Beginner (A2) level English course OR equivalent proficiency demonstrated through our online placement test.
  • Ability to understand and participate in basic English conversations.
  • Access to a computer or tablet with a stable internet connection, webcam, and microphone.

Certification:

Upon successful completion, students receive a Certificate of Achievement from American Expressions Institute, indicating proficiency at the CEFR B1/B2 level.

What kind of topics will be covered in the Intermediate course?

The Intermediate English Fluency course covers a wide range of practical and engaging topics, including:

  • Sharing personal experiences, travel stories, and future ambitions.
  • Discussing work, career paths, and professional communication.
  • Expressing opinions on current events, social issues, and cultural topics.
  • Understanding and giving advice, making suggestions, and problem-solving.
  • Exploring hobbies, lifestyle choices, and health & wellness.
  • Engaging with authentic materials like news articles, short stories, and video clips.

The focus is on developing your ability to communicate effectively in real-world scenarios.

How much speaking practice will I get in this online course?

Speaking practice is a core component of our Intermediate English Fluency course. You will have numerous opportunities to speak through:

  • Live interactive sessions with your instructor and classmates via Zoom.
  • Pair and small group activities during live classes.
  • Role-playing exercises and debates on various topics.
  • Assigned speaking tasks that you can record and submit for feedback.
  • Participation in our optional Conversational English Club for extra practice.

Our goal is to build your confidence and fluency in spoken English.

The American Expressions Institute Academic Team

What if I find the Intermediate course too easy or too difficult after starting?

We strive to ensure every student is placed in the most appropriate course for their current level. We use a placement test and initial assessment to guide this.

However, if after the first week or two of the course, you and your instructor feel that the level is not the best fit, we will discuss options with you. This may include moving to a more suitable course (either higher or lower level, if available and appropriate) or providing additional support materials to help you succeed in your current course. Our priority is your learning progress and satisfaction.
